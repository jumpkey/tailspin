# DRAFT — Letter to PSA Airlines / Envoy Air leadership

**Status: DRAFT for your review. Not sent. Fill bracketed fields; decide
recipient(s) and tone before anything leaves your hands.**

Note on framing: this is written so it can be sent to **PSA** and **Envoy**
either jointly or separately. Because the data treats the two very differently —
PSA over-represented in fragile cells, Envoy under-represented — a fair letter
*says so plainly*. That candor is what keeps it credible to both. If sending
separately, use the operator-specific paragraph marked below.

[Date]

[Name / Title]
[PSA Airlines, Inc. / Envoy Air, Inc.]

Dear [Name],

I am writing as a frequent flyer on American Eagle service and as someone who
works with operational data. I recently completed an analysis of public U.S. DOT
on-time records and want to share it with you directly, in fairness, before it
informs any wider conversation.

The analysis covers **6.15 million flights** across American's nine hubs and 255
spoke airports over the 24 months ending December 2025 (with a season-matched check
into the published 2026 months), scoring route-operator combinations on
cancellation and cascading-delay risk. It uses only public data (BTS On-Time
Performance and NOAA weather) and is fully reproducible.

I want to be transparent about both the method and its limits. It is an
**associational** study of public outcomes — it does not diagnose cause, and it
does not evaluate your crews, maintenance, or stations. Cancellation and delay
outcomes reflect the schedule you are given to fly, aircraft and crew routing,
weather, and air-traffic conditions together. I also want to be clear that the
two wholly-owned American regionals appear **very differently** in the data, and
I think you are each entitled to know exactly where you stand rather than be
grouped under a generic "regional" label.

**[PSA-specific paragraph — use when addressing PSA]**
PSA-operated cells are over-represented among the network's most fragile route-
operator combinations — roughly four times PSA's share of flights, in the worst
5% of cells — with a signature dominated by *cascading* (late-inbound-aircraft)
delay at thin spokes feeding ORD, DFW, and DCA. One corridor I know personally,
DFW–Lafayette, ranks in the worst ~4% of the network when PSA-operated — and PSA
now flies roughly 90% of it (up from ~40% in 2024), with a season-matched check
showing the pattern persisting into the published 2026 months. I raise this not to
assign blame — the schedule and aircraft routing you fly are designed upstream —
but because the pattern is consistent and public, and you would presumably rather
see it first than hear about it later.

**[Envoy-specific paragraph — use when addressing Envoy]**
In fairness, the data shows Envoy-operated cells *under*-represented among the
most fragile combinations — below Envoy's share of flights at every cutoff, with
a markedly lower average fragility score than the other wholly-owned regional. On
the DFW–Lafayette corridor, for example, Envoy service is unremarkable where
another operator's is not (Envoy has, in fact, largely handed that corridor to PSA
since late 2025). I include this because an honest analysis reports the
favorable findings as plainly as the unfavorable ones, and because it is relevant
context for how the overall pattern should — and should not — be read.

My question to both operators is the same one I am putting to American:

> Is systematic schedule-fragility stress-testing performed across the
> hub / spoke / operator / weather grid, and do these specific cells already
> appear on it?

If so, I am glad the work is in hand. If not, a pattern visible from outside with
public data seems worth examining inside. I am happy to share the full
methodology, sources, and route-level results so your teams can check it against
your own records — and the complete source code is publicly available at
[repository URL], so every figure can be reproduced and challenged. I have no
commercial interest; I would like these corridors to improve for the people who
fly them.

Respectfully,

[Your name]
